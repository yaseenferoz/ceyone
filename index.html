Who Are We?

The Ceyone Empowering Society (TCES) is dedicated to educate, empower and sustainable development of underprivileged communities by creating awareness on pressing issues such as importance of community development programme, Skill education and Health & Hygeine. TCES journey began in 2021 when a group of like-minded people from various backgrounds decided to make a difference in the lives of those less privileged than them.



After TCES was established as an NGO on 6th August 2021, it has relentlessly started working in the spheres of sustainable concept of education, health, and women empowerment in under-privileged communities. TCES strongly believes in the power of education as a tool of empowerment and uses it to break taboos and stereotypes. This belief shapes all our programs and the work we do. Renewable & Sustainable energy

TCES supports several efforts to mainstream renewable energy, such as wind and solar, in the overall energy mix of the country. TCES focuses on strengthening policies and regulations that promote renewable energy, developing a flexible electricity grid that is capable of handling high penetration of renewable energy, mobilizing finance for renewable energy projects and assisting state-level agencies to deploy large-scale renewable energy.

Waste management

TCES reduces waste and consumption, manages solid and electronic waste and advocates around materials, waste and consumption. It uses waste as a tool to fight poverty, child labour gender-based violence and exclusion and climate change, while creating green livelihoods.

Our Strategy

Our strategy involves the 3Es, i.e., Engaging, Executing and Empowering



  1. Engaging:To engage rural communities with corporates, young urban India, not-for-profit organizations, and government bodies

  2. Executing:To execute programs, on behalf of our clients, that transforms and has a positive impact on the lives of people living in these communities

  3. Empowering:Through the execution of the programmes, we aim to empower our rural communities/urban youth.
